Content Taxonomy

A structured framework for planning, tagging, and scaling skin health content. Each article should be assigned a Pillar, Content Type, Search Intent, and Audience Segment. This enables consistent taxonomy across CMS, SEO, and editorial planning.

Dimension 1 — Content Type

What form does the content take? This determines structure, word count, and editorial format.

Content TypeDescriptionTypical LengthExamples
Condition OverviewComprehensive explainer covering causes, symptoms, diagnosis, and treatment options for a specific skin condition.1,500–2,500wWhat is eczema?, Understanding psoriasis, Melanoma explained
Symptom GuideHelps readers identify whether their symptoms match a condition. Often structured with comparison tables.800–1,500wIs this rash eczema or psoriasis?, Signs of skin cancer to watch for
Treatment & ManagementExplores medical and self-management options for a condition. Distinguishes GP, dermatologist, and at-home approaches.1,000–2,000wHow is psoriasis treated?, Managing eczema flare-ups
Prevention & Risk ReductionEvidence-based guidance on reducing the risk of developing or worsening a skin condition.800–1,400wHow to reduce skin cancer risk, Preventing eczema triggers
When to See a DoctorDecision-support content that helps readers know when self-management is insufficient and professional care is needed.600–1,000wWhen to see a GP about a mole, Should I see a dermatologist?
Explainer / FAQAnswers specific, high-intent questions in a structured Q&A or short explainer format.400–900wWhat causes hives?, Can stress cause eczema?, Is psoriasis contagious?
Living WithPractical, empathetic content for people managing a chronic skin condition in daily life.900–1,800wLiving with psoriasis at work, Eczema and mental health, Managing rosacea in summer
Research & NewsPlain-language summaries of new studies, treatment advances, or policy changes relevant to skin health.500–1,000wNew biologic treatments for psoriasis, Study links gut health to eczema
Statistics & PrevalenceData-led content on how common a condition is in Australia, who it affects, and health system impact.800–1,500wSkin cancer statistics in Australia, How many Australians have eczema?
Care Pathway GuideExplains how the Australian health system handles a skin condition — from GP referral to specialist care and follow-up.900–1,600wGetting a skin cancer check in Australia, Seeing a dermatologist on a referral

Why is the user searching? Aligning content to intent is the strongest driver of organic performance.

IntentUser GoalSignal KeywordsBest Content Types

Dimension 3 — Audience Segment

Who is the primary reader? Content should be calibrated in language complexity, empathy, and clinical depth accordingly.

SegmentProfileKey Content NeedsTone
Newly DiagnosedRecently told they have a skin condition; high anxiety, low knowledge baseClear explanations, what to expect, who to see, emotional reassuranceWarm, plain-language, reassuring
Long-term Condition ManagerLiving with a chronic condition (psoriasis, eczema, rosacea) for yearsFlare management, new treatments, living well, mental health supportPeer-like, practical, evidence-informed
Parent / CarerManaging a child's or dependent's skin healthPaediatric-specific advice, safe treatments, school/daycare guidanceEmpathetic, action-oriented, safety-focused
Prevention-SeekerMotivated by sun safety, skin cancer screening, or general skin wellnessRisk factors, screening schedules, lifestyle advice, Australian-specific UV dataEmpowering, evidence-based, practical
Symptom ResearcherHas noticed a new symptom or change and is self-triaging before seeing a doctorSymptom identification, urgency signals, when to actClear, non-alarmist, action-guiding
Older Adults (55+)Increased risk of skin cancers, ageing-related skin changes, chronic conditionsSkin cancer vigilance, age-related conditions, Medicare/specialist pathwaysRespectful, authoritative, Australian-context aware
